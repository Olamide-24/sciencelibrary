Physics

Discover the fundamental laws that govern the universe

Chapter 1: Mechanics

Mechanics is the branch of physics dealing with the motion of bodies under the action of forces.

1.1 Newton's Laws of Motion

Newton's laws of motion are three physical laws that laid the foundation for classical mechanics.

First Law: F = 0 ⇒ a = 0 (Object at rest stays at rest)
Second Law: F = ma
Third Law: F₁₂ = -F₂₁ (Action-Reaction)

1.2 Energy and Work

Work is done when a force causes displacement. Energy is the capacity to do work.

Work: W = F × d × cos(θ)
Kinetic Energy: KE = ½mv²
Potential Energy: PE = mgh

Chapter 2: Electricity

Electricity is the set of physical phenomena associated with the presence and motion of matter that has a property of electric charge.

2.1 Ohm's Law

Ohm's law states that the current through a conductor between two points is directly proportional to the voltage across the two points.

V = IR
Where V = voltage, I = current, R = resistance

2.2 Electric Circuits

An electric circuit is a path in which electrons from a voltage or current source flow.

Chapter 3: Waves and Optics

Waves are disturbances that transfer energy through matter or space.

3.1 Wave Properties

Waves are characterized by wavelength, frequency, period, and amplitude.

Wave Speed: v = fλ
Where f = frequency, λ = wavelength
