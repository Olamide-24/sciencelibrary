English Language

Grammar, Comprehension, and Composition

Chapter 1: Parts of Speech

Understanding the building blocks of English sentences.

1.1 Nouns

Nouns are naming words. They name people, places, things, or ideas.

  • Proper nouns: Specific names (London, John)
  • Common nouns: General names (city, boy)
  • Abstract nouns: Ideas (love, freedom)

1.2 Verbs

Verbs express action or state of being.

  • Action verbs: run, think, write
  • Linking verbs: am, is, are, was, were
  • Helping verbs: have, do, will, can

Chapter 2: Tenses

Verb tenses indicate time of action.

2.1 Present Tense

Used for current actions or habits.

Subject + verb (s/es for third person)

2.2 Past Tense

Used for completed actions.

Subject + verb-ed (regular) / irregular form

Chapter 3: Comprehension Skills

Strategies for understanding written texts.

3.1 Skimming and Scanning

Skimming for main ideas, scanning for specific information.

3.2 Inference

Reading between the lines to understand implied meaning.
