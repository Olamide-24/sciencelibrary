Further Mathematics

Advanced Calculus, Statistics, and Complex Numbers

Chapter 1: Complex Numbers

Numbers beyond the real number line.

1.1 Definition

Complex numbers have the form a + bi, where i² = -1.

i = √(-1)

1.2 Operations

  • Addition: (a+bi) + (c+di) = (a+c) + (b+d)i
  • Multiplication: Use FOIL and i² = -1
  • Conjugate: a + bi → a - bi

Chapter 2: Advanced Calculus

Advanced techniques in differentiation and integration.

2.1 Implicit Differentiation

Differentiating functions not explicitly defined.

dy/dx = - (∂F/∂x) / (∂F/∂y)

2.2 Parametric Equations

Curves defined by x = f(t), y = g(t).

dy/dx = (dy/dt) / (dx/dt)

Chapter 3: Statistics

Advanced statistical methods and probability.

3.1 Probability Distributions

  • Binomial: Fixed trials, two outcomes
  • Normal: Bell curve, continuous data
  • Poisson: Rare events

3.2 Hypothesis Testing

Testing claims using statistical evidence.

H₀: μ = μ₀ vs H₁: μ ≠ μ₀
