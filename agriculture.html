Agriculture Science

Crop Science, Animal Husbandry, and Agricultural Economics

Chapter 1: Crop Production

Fundamentals of growing crops for food and other uses.

1.1 Factors Affecting Crop Growth

  • Climate: Temperature, rainfall, sunlight
  • Soil: Type, pH, nutrients, drainage
  • Water: Irrigation and rainfall requirements
  • Seeds: Quality, variety, viability

1.2 Farming Systems

  • Subsistence farming: Small scale, family consumption
  • Commercial farming: Large scale, for sale
  • Mixed farming: Crops and livestock together

Chapter 2: Soil Science

Understanding soil properties and management.

2.1 Soil Composition

  • Mineral particles: Sand, silt, clay
  • Organic matter: Decayed plant and animal material
  • Water and air: Essential for root growth
  • Living organisms: Bacteria, fungi, earthworms

2.2 Soil Fertility

Maintaining nutrients for optimal plant growth.

N-P-K: Nitrogen, Phosphorus, Potassium

Raising animals for food, fiber, and other products.

3.1 livestock Management

  • Cattle: Beef and dairy production
  • Poultry: Chickens for meat and eggs
  • Small ruminants: Goats and sheep

Feed requirements for different classes of livestock.
